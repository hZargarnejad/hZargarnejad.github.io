About Me

Education and Work Background

I am Hanif Zargar, Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Texas A&M University, with a specialized background in Computational Fluid Dynamics, turbulence modeling, and multiphase flow physics. My work focuses on developing high-fidelity simulation tools to address complex fluid and particle dynamics relevant to both engineering applications and natural phenomena.

Over the past several years, I have led and contributed to research projects involving the simulation of particle-laden flows, gas-liquid interactions, and radiation-driven multiphase instabilities. I have extensive experience with open-source platforms such as OpenFOAM and DEAL.II, as well as adapting large-scale scientific codes like FLASH for custom, high-performance simulations. My programming expertise includes Fortran, C++, and Python, which I apply not only to solver development but also to building automated post-processing and data visualization tools for large, adaptive mesh datasets.

In addition to my core research, I have worked on side projects such as simulating spray dynamics and particle sedimentation in confined geometries, providing insights into experimental challenges like early-time particle clustering. This experience has strengthened my ability to bridge experimental observations with numerical modeling to solve practical engineering problems.

Beyond technical development, I am passionate about interdisciplinary collaboration, whether in engineering, astrophysics, or applied research. I enjoy mentoring students, communicating complex ideas clearly, and contributing to research teams that tackle challenging, real-world problems through simulation and computational science.
